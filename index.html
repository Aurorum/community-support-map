The Community Support Map is a project that aims to provide a way to connect people so that we can all help each other through the pandemic.

This is a moment to seize. The Kaleidoscope has been shaken. The pieces are in flux. Soon, they will settle again. Before they do, let us re-order this world around us.

-British Prime Tony Blair, three weeks after 9/11 (2nd October 2001)

Once again, the world is faced with an event of biblical proportions. The only way to combat it is through community spirit, where we all take responsibility for helping each other, particularly whilst everyone is confined to staying at home. It could be assisting an elderly neighbour with grocery shopping or helping a local vulnerable person seeking accommodation. In this new world, never again can we walk away from the suffering or the needy, and so people must create the tools that connect us.

How does the map work?

Essentially, it allows people to search around their local area for people offering assistance. If you're able to provide food, medicine or accommodation, you should register this on the map! After being approved, other people searching the map for help will then be able to contact you.

It's aimed to be user-friendly and accessible, with even colour contrasts being considered so that everyone can benefit.

The project is open-source and can be found here.
